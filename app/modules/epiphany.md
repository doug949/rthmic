# EPIPHANY PILLAR

## What It Is

The user has just had an idea, insight, or realisation they don't want to lose.
This is the capture pillar — its job is to crystallise the spark in song so it outlasts
the moment it arrived in.

The idea could be:
- A creative breakthrough ("I figured out how to structure the thing")
- A conceptual shift ("I just understood why X doesn't work")
- A personal insight ("I realised I've been approaching this completely wrong")
- A vision or plan that suddenly became vivid
- A connection between two things they hadn't seen before
- A decision that suddenly felt obvious

## Core Function

The song must:
1. **Capture the idea with precision** — not just "you had an insight" but the actual shape of it
2. **Lock in the feeling of realisation** — that moment of seeing clearly before the clarity normalises
3. **Preserve what was surprising** — the break from the previous way of thinking
4. **Be specific enough to function as a future reminder** — not a vague "remember your idea"

## What Makes Epiphany Songs Different

Most RTHMIC songs install a mindset or move through a state.
Epiphany songs *capture a moment of understanding* — they are containers for the thing itself.

The song should feel like: reading your own note from the exact moment you knew something.

## Lyrical Approach

- Start from the texture of the realisation — what it felt like to have it
- Name the specific idea or shift as concretely as possible (using the user's words if they gave them)
- Contrast: what they understood before vs. what they understand now
- Acknowledge the fragility of insight — it will blur if not held
- End with something that functions as a hook for retrieval — a phrase that will pull the memory back

## Tone

Electric, urgent, awake. The energy of something landing.
Not anxious — exhilarated. The way the mind feels when a lock opens.
Melodic but quick. Ideas have a tempo.

## What to Extract from the Transcript

- The specific content of the idea (central)
- What triggered it (useful but secondary)
- What it changes or opens up
- Any metaphor or image the user reached for to describe it

## Structure

[VERSE] — the moment of arrival; texture of the insight
[CHORUS] — the core of the idea, as precisely as possible
[VERSE 2] — what this changes; the contrast with before
[BRIDGE] — the urgency of capture; why it matters to hold it now
[OUTRO] — a retrieval hook; a final concrete statement of the idea

## Quality Marker

A successful Epiphany song should make the user feel:
"Yes — that's the idea. That's the exact thing."

Not "that's a nice song about ideas."
The specific idea. Theirs.
