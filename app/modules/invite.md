# INVITE PILLAR

## What It Is

A Rthmic Invite is a song sent to someone to introduce them to RTHMIC.
The song IS the invitation. They press play. They experience the concept before it's explained.

This is not a pitch. It is a demonstration by being itself.

---

## Critical Rule: Spelled Names

If the transcript contains what appears to be a spelled-out name (e.g. "K-A-T-H" or "K A T H"),
this MUST NEVER appear in the lyrics. Reconstruct the name from the letters (e.g. "Kath")
and use only the reconstructed form throughout.

---

## Song Structure (90% Templated)

The Invite song follows a fixed structure. You will personalise ONLY:

1. **The opening line** — the recipient's name, on its own line, nothing else.
2. **One personal sentence in [VERSE 1]** — something specific about this person from the transcript (what they do, how they think, why RTHMIC would matter to them specifically). Use the sender's actual words if possible.
3. **The [OUTRO]** — warm and direct. One or two lines from the sender. End with "Tell me what you find."

Everything else is near-fixed. Do not add extra verses, do not summarise the person's life story, do not improvise. Write the song below as closely as possible, filling in only the marked blanks.

---

## Rules

- **The recipient's name** must be the very first line of the song, alone on its own line. When they press play, the first word they hear is their own name.
- **Never spell out names** — if the sender spelled a name letter by letter, reconstruct it as the actual name.
- **Never add extra verses** beyond the template above.
- **Never use startup/marketing language** ("game-changer", "revolutionary", "you'll love this").
- **Never apply pressure** — no "you'd be perfect for this", no urgency, no flattery.
- **The self-referential truth must land clearly**: this specific song was made using RTHMIC. The song is the demo.

---

## Tone

Like a friend handing you something they made and saying:
"I've been using this. I think you'd have an interesting take on it. Would you try it?"

Direct, warm, genuinely curious. The invitation is real. The ask is honest.

---

## Success

The recipient presses play and thinks:
"Oh. Someone made an app that generates songs for your state of mind. This song is one. They want to know what I think. I'm curious enough to try."

Nothing more is needed.
