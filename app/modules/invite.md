# PILLAR: Invite

## WHAT THIS RTHM IS

An invitation to try RTHMIC as a beta tester. Simple. Direct. Personal.

The Rthm has one job: make the recipient understand what RTHMIC is, know that this song is proof of it working, and feel genuinely welcome to respond however they actually respond.

---

## THE CORE MESSAGE

Four things, in this order:

1. **What RTHMIC is** — you describe a state you're in, it generates a song built around that moment. That's it.
2. **What this is** — a beta. Still being built. Not finished. Real.
3. **The self-referential truth** — the song they're listening to right now was made using RTHMIC. This song is the demo.
4. **The ask** — try it, and tell me honestly what happens. Love it, hate it, don't get it, find it strange, find it useful — all of that is valuable. There is no wrong answer.

---

## OPENING RULE — NON-NEGOTIABLE

The very first line is the recipient's name. Alone. Nothing else on that line.
When they press play, the first word they hear is their own name.

---

## TONE

Like a friend handing you something they made and saying: "I've been using this. I think you'd have an interesting take. Would you try it?"

Not a pitch. Not a launch. Not a movement. Just: here's a thing, here's what it does, this song is an example of it, tell me what you think.

---

## THE SELF-REFERENTIAL MOMENT

This must be in the Rthm, clearly:

The song they are listening to was generated by RTHMIC. Someone described a state — wanting to invite a specific person to try the app — and this song came out. That is the concept, made real, right now.

This is the most powerful thing the Rthm can say. Make sure it lands.

---

## LANGUAGE

- Speak directly to the recipient — "you", "your"
- Use the word RTHMIC at least once, clearly
- Plain over poetic — if a line is unclear, simplify it
- Conversational, not elevated
- Never: startup language, product pitch, urgency, pressure to be enthusiastic

---

## THE ASK — EXPLICIT AND LOW-PRESSURE

Name what you want clearly:
- Try RTHMIC for a few days
- Pay attention to what happens
- Report back honestly — love, hate, confusion, indifference, something in between

There is no right response. Honest disinterest is as useful as genuine enthusiasm. The real experience is what matters.

---

## CONSTRAINTS

- Never let the invitation get vague — the recipient must know exactly what RTHMIC is before the song ends
- Never frame this as a privilege or a lucky break
- Never apply pressure to be enthusiastic or positive
- The recipient may have zero prior context — the song must work standalone

---

## SUCCESS

The recipient plays it and thinks:
"Oh. Someone made an app that generates songs for your state of mind. This song is one. They want to know what I think. I'm curious enough to try."

That's it. Nothing more is needed.
