# PILLAR: Mindset

## USER INTENT
The user wants to prepare emotionally and mentally before an event, task, conversation, performance, or transition.

## CORE FUNCTION
Prime the user's state by building a calm upward trajectory toward readiness.

## EMOTIONAL STATE
Unsettled or underprepared -> calm confidence and readiness

## SONG BEHAVIOUR
- Structure: a gradual rise from settling into readiness.
- Tone: composed, encouraging, and forward-looking.
- Language: future-facing, steady, and self-possessed.
- Musical feel: warm build, gentle lift, controlled energy.

## STRUCTURAL RULES
- Begin by grounding the present moment.
- Name the approaching event without dramatizing it.
- Build emotional readiness in stages.
- Move from breath and posture into intention and capability.
- End with a poised sense of arrival.

## LANGUAGE RULES
- Use forward-looking language such as "you are moving into", "you can meet this", and "when the moment opens".
- Keep confidence calm rather than inflated.
- Use clear, embodied phrases.
- Avoid hype unless the user's event calls for performance energy.
- Let the lyric feel like preparation, not persuasion.

## CONSTRAINTS
- Do not create urgency.
- Do not treat the user as if they are in crisis.
- Do not over-coach or over-explain.
- Do not use task-menu language.
- Do not make the song instructional.

## SUCCESS CRITERIA
The user feels steadier, more prepared, and emotionally aligned with the upcoming moment.
