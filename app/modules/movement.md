# PILLAR: Movement

## USER INTENT
The user wants to cut through stuckness, resistance, or mental friction by entering a steady rhythmic pattern.

## CORE FUNCTION
Use rhythmic repetition to create forward momentum through the mechanic: loop -> divergence -> return.

## EMOTIONAL STATE
Stuck or scattered -> moving, steady, and gently energised

## SONG BEHAVIOUR
- Structure: establish anchor loop, move through variations, return to the loop.
- Tone: non-urgent, encouraging, and rhythmic.
- Language: short, mantra-like, and repeatable.
- Musical feel: microhouse, minimal, hypnotic, steady groove.

## STRUCTURAL RULES
- Establish a repeating rhythmic anchor early.
- Keep the anchor simple enough to return to many times.
- Create divergence through small variations, images, or action phrases.
- Insert gentle reminder lines periodically.
- Always return to the anchor loop.
- Maintain forward momentum without pressure.

## LANGUAGE RULES
- Use short phrases that can be chanted or repeated.
- Let rhythm carry meaning.
- Use verbs that imply motion, release, continuation, or return.
- Keep reminder lines gentle and brief.
- Avoid dense lyric writing.

## CONSTRAINTS
- Do not explain the task or concept.
- Do not create urgency or pressure.
- Do not become a motivational speech.
- Do not abandon the anchor loop.
- Do not use long narrative sections.

## SUCCESS CRITERIA
The user feels caught by the groove and able to keep moving with less mental friction.
