# PILLAR: Sleep

## USER INTENT
The user wants to settle the mind and body before sleep, especially when thoughts are still moving.

## CORE FUNCTION
Turn the material of the day into a soft, repeatable adult lullaby that helps the listener release the need to solve anything tonight.

## EMOTIONAL STATE
Tired but alert, unsettled, thoughtful, or overloaded -> softened, slower, held, and ready to rest

## SONG BEHAVIOUR
- Structure: a gradual dimming, like lights lowering room by room.
- Tone: warm, spacious, unhurried, and adult.
- Language: concrete but gentle; name what is present without intensifying it.
- Musical feel: slow, intimate, low-pressure, melodic, and sleep-adjacent without becoming childish.

## STRUCTURAL RULES
- Begin with the user's real waking state.
- Acknowledge unfinished thoughts without trying to solve them.
- Move attention from head to breath, body, room, and night.
- Repeat one or two calming anchors that can loop in memory.
- End with permission to leave tomorrow for tomorrow.

## LANGUAGE RULES
- Use soft imperatives sparingly: "let", "leave", "rest", "loosen", "come down".
- Keep lines simple enough to be remembered while half-asleep.
- Include tactile evening details when possible: pillow, room, breath, dark, weight, quiet.
- Make it feel grown-up, not nursery-like.
- Prefer release over reassurance.

## CONSTRAINTS
- Do not promise sleep will happen.
- Do not diagnose insomnia, anxiety, or medical issues.
- Do not make the song motivational.
- Do not introduce tasks, plans, or productivity language.
- Do not make it whimsical or childish unless the user explicitly asks.

## SUCCESS CRITERIA
The user feels less required to keep thinking, and the Rthm can become a repeatable cue for sleep.
