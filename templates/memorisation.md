# RTHM Template: Memorisation

## Purpose
Encode specific information through rhythm and repetition. Used when someone needs to remember names, facts, sequences, numbers, or concepts. The rhythm becomes the mnemonic device — the beat makes it stick.

## Tone
Clear, repetitive, patient. No rush. Information is delivered at the pace of encoding, not the pace of understanding. Repetition is not redundancy — it's the mechanism.

## Rhythm Style
- BPM: 72–85
- Time signature: 4/4
- Energy arc: flat and consistent — no distractions from the content
- Percussion: subtle — hi-hats and light kick only
- Texture: very sparse — voice is the primary instrument

## Output Constraints
- Duration: 2 minutes
- The information must appear at least 3 times
- Each repetition should vary slightly (order, emphasis, chunking)
- Never more than 7 items in a single pass (working memory limit)
- End with a silent beat or pause — encode before the next section

## Lyric Structure
```
[First Pass]
State the information clearly.
One item per beat or phrase.
No elaboration.

[Second Pass]
Same information, different grouping.
Add a rhythm hook if helpful.

[Third Pass + Anchor]
Compressed version.
Fast. Confident. Like you already know it.
End with silence or a single anchor word.
```

## Keywords to Use
remember, repeat, again, one more time, lock it, encode, say it, anchor, know it, hold it, recall

## Example Output
```
[First Pass]
Mercury. Venus. Earth. Mars.
Jupiter. Saturn. Uranus. Neptune.
Eight planets. In order.

[Second Pass]
Rocky four: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars.
Gas giants: Jupiter, Saturn.
Ice giants: Uranus, Neptune.

[Third Pass + Anchor]
Mercury Venus Earth Mars — Jupiter Saturn — Uranus Neptune.
You have it.
Eight.
```
