# RTHM Template: Mindset

## Purpose
Shift an emotional or psychological state. Used when someone is anxious, overwhelmed, demotivated, fearful, or stuck in a loop. The track doesn't instruct — it moves. The rhythm is the intervention.

## Tone
Warm but not soft. Honest — it doesn't pretend things are fine. It acknowledges the state and walks through it, not around it. The shift happens gradually, tracked by the music's arc.

## Rhythm Style
- BPM: 70 → 95 (builds through the track)
- Time signature: 4/4 or 6/8
- Energy arc: starts low, mirrors the stuck state, lifts through the middle, resolves at 85–90%
- Percussion: starts sparse (brushes), adds weight progressively
- Texture: starts thin, adds layers — mirrors internal state shift

## Output Constraints
- Duration: 2 minutes
- Must acknowledge the current state honestly in the first section
- The turn must happen naturally — not forced positivity
- End grounded, not euphoric
- No toxic optimism. No "you've got this" clichés.

## Lyric Structure
```
[Acknowledge]
Name what's happening right now.
Don't fix it yet. Just name it.
2–4 lines.

[Turn]
The moment the state begins to shift.
Not resolution — movement.
3–5 lines.

[Ground]
Where you land.
Not solved, but stable.
2–3 lines.
```

## Keywords to Use
feel, heavy, here, still, breath, through, shift, steady, grounded, clear, moving, enough, present, real

## Example Output
```
[Acknowledge]
Heavy. That's the word.
Everything's too big right now.
That's real. Don't argue with it.

[Turn]
But heavy things can still move.
One breath.
You're not stuck — you're loading.
The next step isn't far.
It's just the next step.

[Ground]
You're here. That's enough for now.
The rest can wait one more minute.
Move when you're ready.
You will be.
```
